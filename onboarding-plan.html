Sample deliverable · 90-day onboarding plan

Onboarding a plaintiff firm to Eve in 90 days

A phase-by-phase plan to take a mid-size plaintiff firm from signed contract to confident daily use — kickoff and configuration, role-based training, a controlled go-live, and a 90-day adoption review — with owners and the success metrics I’d track at each stage.

About this sample

Independent work sample built only from Eve’s public product information; not affiliated with, authorized by, or endorsed by Eve. Timelines, thresholds, and targets are illustrative and would be set with the firm and the Eve team on the real engagement.

The arc

Three phases, one goal: real utilization

The plan assumes a firm adopting Eve across intake, drafting, and case work. Each phase has an exit condition — you don’t move on until it’s met.

  1. Days 0–15 — Kickoff & configuration. Align on success, map the firm’s workflow, and configure Eve to it. Exit: success plan signed, intake criteria configured, case system connected.
  2. Days 16–45 — Training & go-live. Train each role, then go live on a controlled set of matters. Exit: all users trained; first AI-handled intakes and first drafted document reviewed and sent.
  3. Days 46–90 — Adoption & review. Drive utilization, remove friction, expand use, and review value at day 90. Exit: utilization targets met; QBR and expansion plan delivered.

Phase 1 · Days 0–15

Kickoff & configuration

Get the firm aligned and the platform shaped to how they actually work.

01

Kickoff & success plan

Align stakeholders — managing partner, intake lead, paralegal/ops, IT — on goals, timeline, and the metrics that define success.

02

Workflow mapping

Map the firm’s intake-to-resolution flow and pinpoint where Eve fits: intake qualification, drafting, records review, and case analysis.

03

Configuration

Set the firm’s intake and qualification criteria, connect Eve to the case-management system, and tune drafting to the firm’s tone and templates.

04

Data & access review

Confirm permissions, data handling, and who can see what — so the firm is comfortable before any live matter touches the platform.

Phase 2 · Days 16–45

Training & go-live

Train for the job each role actually does, then go live where the value is clearest.

1 Role-based training

Intake staff: how the AI intake specialist qualifies and scores leads, when to take a warm transfer, and how qualified matters flow into the case system.

Attorneys: reviewing AI-drafted demand letters, complaints, and charges; using case analysis and valuation to inform strategy.

Paralegals: records and case-file review, fact extraction, and document workflows.

Formats: live role sessions, short quick-reference guides, and recordings for new hires — so training outlives the kickoff.

2 Controlled go-live

Go live on a defined slice first — one practice area or matter type — so wins are visible and issues are contained. Pair every user with the habit that protects trust: review AI output against the source before relying on it.

3 First value

Target concrete early wins: the first AI-handled intakes converting to signed matters, and the first AI-drafted demand letter reviewed and sent — proof the firm can feel in weeks, not quarters.

Phase 3 · Days 46–90

Adoption & 90-day review

Turn early use into habit, expand, and prove the value.

01

Drive utilization

Usage nudges, standing office hours, and fast removal of the friction that stalls rollout — the unglamorous work that decides adoption.

02

Expand use

Roll out to more matter types and users once the first slice is sticking; add use cases the firm hadn’t considered.

03

90-day value review

A QBR against the success plan: utilization vs. target, feature adoption, and firm-reported outcomes like added capacity or faster drafting.

04

Plan the next quarter

Agree the expansion path and the next set of outcomes — turning a successful onboarding into a growing account.

Risk watch

Where onboarding stalls — and how I’d catch it

The four I watch for

Champion turnover — name a backup champion at kickoff. Mis-set intake criteria — review qualification results in week one and tune. Attorney distrust of AI drafts — coach the review-against-source habit and show citations/facts behind a draft. Case-system sync issues — validate the integration before go-live, and partner with Eve’s product and engineering teams to resolve anything substantive rather than letting it quietly stall adoption.
