About

I have run a plaintiff firm from the inside

For more than a decade I ran the daily operations of a boutique plaintiff-side employment and corporate-law firm — as the only paralegal, with no secretarial support, I handled the practice end to end: intake, client communication, case and matter management, discovery, document production, vendor and software coordination, and billing. I know how a firm actually runs, where the friction lives, and what it takes to get busy legal professionals to trust and adopt a new way of working.

More recently I have done that adoption work for AI. As a senior litigation paralegal I joined my firm’s AI committee and an enterprise pilot integrating tools like Microsoft Copilot and Filevine’s Lois into real legal workflows — testing them against live tasks (including close QC of AI-generated medical chronologies), evaluating output, documenting workflows, and co-leading training for colleagues from staff to partners. My graduate training in biomedical visualization is, at its core, about making complex things understandable. This fall I begin the University of Hawaiʻi Executive J.D. program focused on AI law and governance.
