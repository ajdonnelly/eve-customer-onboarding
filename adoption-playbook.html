Sample deliverable · Onboarding & adoption playbook

The repeatable framework behind the plan

A single 90-day plan is one engagement; a playbook is how you run dozens without losing the thread. This is the operating model I’d use to onboard and drive adoption across a book of plaintiff firms on Eve — the principles, the lifecycle, the training library, and how I’d know which accounts are healthy.

About this sample

Independent work sample built only from Eve’s public product information; not affiliated with or endorsed by Eve. Framework and thresholds are illustrative.

Principles

Five principles that travel to every firm

01

Start from the workflow

Configure to how the firm actually runs a matter — not a generic feature tour.

02

Train by role

Intake, attorneys, and paralegals each get training for the job they actually do.

03

Protect trust

Coach the review-against-source habit on every AI draft and extraction, so confidence is earned, not assumed.

04

Measure value

Tie every rollout to time-to-value, utilization, and an outcome the firm cares about.

05 · Close the loop

Carry what firms struggle with and ask for back to product — the onboarding function sees friction and feature gaps before anyone else, and that feedback is part of the job.

Lifecycle

From signed to advocate

The stages each firm moves through, and what “done” looks like at each.

  1. Onboard. Kickoff, configuration, and a signed success plan.
  2. Activate. Every role trained; first AI intakes and first drafted document live.
  3. Adopt. Utilization targets met across the firm’s core matter types.
  4. Expand. New users, new matter types, new use cases.
  5. Advocate. A measurable ROI story the firm is willing to share.
